01 / ABOUT

From mechanical systems to embedded firmware.

My background spans aerospace controls, mechanical engineering, industrial systems and firmware. That experience helps me approach embedded products from both system and software perspectives.

I began with control engineering at National Cheng Kung University and later worked across semiconductor equipment, mechanical design, robotics and industrial systems. Today, I develop and troubleshoot firmware for industrial control products.

My current focus is strengthening low-level C, STM32 and RTOS fundamentals while building practical Embedded Linux and Buildroot experience. My long-term direction is BSP and Linux device-driver development.
